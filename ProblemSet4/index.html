A bit about each album:

Modus Vivendi, by 070 Shake

070 Shake's debut studio album is an exploration of alternative pop. There is a large emphasis on synthesis and processed audio.

I consider this album inspiration for the music I like to make as well as a catalyst for me making my own music.

Igor, by Tyler, The Creator

This Grammy award winning album was produced entirely by Tyler, the Creator and is a modern staple for hip hop albums.

Every time I listen to this album, I appreciate it more. The production and songwriting is brilliant and is a gold standard.

Mr Morale & the Big Steppers, by Kendrick Lamar

Kendrick Lamar's most recent entry into his near-perfect catalog, Mr. Morale focuses less on external issues and more on internal struggles.

This album has become my go-to when I'm feeling in need of self-reflection.

The Forever Story, by JID

JID's first solo project in four years, The Forever Story fuses elements from many genres to create an entirely unique sonic palette.

As a creative, I appreciate how much this album has a distinct sound: every song belongs within the project.

Heaven or Hell, by Don Toliver

Don Toliver flexes his incredible soul-inspired vocals on a tracklist that contains hit after hit.

I love Don's voice, especially on beats like these.

Another album you should check out!
